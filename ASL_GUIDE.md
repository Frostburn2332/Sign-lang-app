# ASL Alphabet Detection Guide

This application detects American Sign Language (ASL) alphabet gestures in real-time using your webcam.

## How to Use

1. **Navigate to the Detect page** from the navigation menu
2. **Allow camera access** when prompted
3. **Position your hand** in front of the camera in a well-lit environment
4. **Make ASL alphabet signs** and see them detected in real-time

## ASL Alphabet Reference

### A - Closed fist with thumb on the side
- Make a fist
- Place thumb against the side of your index finger

### B - Flat hand with fingers together
- Extend all four fingers upward together
- Tuck thumb across your palm

### C - Curved hand forming C shape
- Curve all fingers to form the letter C
- Keep thumb separated from fingers

### D - Index finger pointing up
- Point index finger straight up
- Curl other fingers with thumb touching middle finger

### E - All fingers curled down
- Curl all fingers tightly
- Tuck thumb under fingers

### F - Index and thumb forming circle
- Touch index finger tip to thumb tip
- Extend other three fingers upward

### G - Index finger pointing sideways
- Point index finger horizontally
- Thumb pointing up

### H - Index and middle fingers together horizontally
- Extend index and middle fingers together
- Point them horizontally to the side

### I - Pinky finger up
- Extend only your pinky finger
- Keep other fingers curled down

### J - Pinky finger drawing J
- Start with pinky up (like I)
- Draw a J shape in the air

### K - Index and middle up in V, thumb between
- Make a V with index and middle fingers
- Place thumb between them

### L - Index up, thumb out at 90Â°
- Point index finger up
- Extend thumb out to the side (L shape)

### M - Three fingers over thumb
- Curl index, middle, and ring fingers over thumb
- Keep pinky extended

### N - Two fingers over thumb
- Curl index and middle fingers over thumb
- Keep ring and pinky extended

### O - Fingers forming circle
- Touch all fingertips to thumb
- Form a circle/O shape

### P - Index pointing down, middle horizontal
- Point index finger downward
- Middle finger points horizontally
- Thumb between them

### Q - Similar to P but both pointing down
- Both index and thumb point downward

### R - Index and middle fingers crossed
- Cross index and middle fingers
- Keep other fingers curled

### S - Fist with thumb over fingers
- Make a fist
- Place thumb over your fingers (not on the side)

### T - Thumb between index and middle
- Make a fist
- Tuck thumb between index and middle fingers

### U - Index and middle up together
- Extend index and middle fingers together
- Keep them parallel and touching

### V - Index and middle separated (Peace sign)
- Extend index and middle fingers
- Separate them to form a V

### W - Three fingers up
- Extend index, middle, and ring fingers
- Keep them together

### X - Index finger hooked/bent
- Bend index finger at the middle joint
- Keep other fingers curled

### Y - Thumb and pinky out (Shaka)
- Extend thumb and pinky
- Curl other fingers down

### Z - Index finger draws Z
- Point index finger
- Draw the letter Z in the air

## Tips for Better Detection

1. **Lighting**: Ensure good, even lighting on your hand
2. **Background**: Use a plain, contrasting background
3. **Distance**: Keep your hand 1-2 feet from the camera
4. **Clarity**: Make clear, distinct gestures
5. **Stillness**: Hold each sign steady for 1-2 seconds
6. **Hand Orientation**: Face your palm toward the camera for most signs

## Learning Resources

Visit the **Learn** page to see visual examples of each letter and practice making the signs!

## Common Issues

- **"Unknown" detection**: Make sure your hand is clearly visible and well-lit
- **Wrong letter detected**: Check your hand position matches the reference images
- **No detection**: Ensure camera permissions are granted and hand is in frame

## Note on Dynamic Signs

Some letters (J, Z) require motion to be fully accurate. The app detects their static starting positions and indicates this with "(static)" in the detection label.
